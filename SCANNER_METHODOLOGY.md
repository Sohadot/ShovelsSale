# Scanner Methodology — ShovelsSale.com

## Purpose

The Shovel Economy Scanner is an applied classification instrument for reading gold-rush markets through structural position rather than surface narrative.

Its purpose is to help users distinguish whether an asset, company, product, or market participant behaves primarily as a Miner, Shovel, Gatekeeper, Hybrid, or Unclear / Early Signal within the Shovel Economy Framework.

The scanner supports the broader ShovelsSale.com reference system:

- `/framework/` defines the structural taxonomy.
- `/scanner/` applies the taxonomy as an interactive classification instrument.
- `/dispatch/` preserves applied case records that interpret real market structures.

## Classification Roles

### Miner

A Miner competes directly for the visible prize inside a rush.

Miner positions are usually exposed to narrative pressure, specific outcome risk, product competition, timing risk, and the need to be correct about a narrower winning scenario.

Miner does not mean weak or unimportant. It means the position depends more directly on visible competition and specific execution outcomes.

### Shovel

A Shovel supplies tools, infrastructure, systems, services, or operational capacity used by many participants in the rush.

Shovel positions may benefit from broad participation rather than needing one visible winner to dominate. They often sit beneath the competition and capture demand created by the race itself.

### Gatekeeper

A Gatekeeper controls access, standards, permissions, distribution, pricing rails, scarce capacity, or system rules.

Gatekeeper positions are structurally powerful because they may shape who can participate, how participation occurs, and what constraints the market must operate within.

### Hybrid

A Hybrid combines more than one role.

An asset may behave like a Shovel in one part of the market while also competing like a Miner elsewhere. It may also begin as infrastructure and move toward Gatekeeper behavior as its control over access, standards, or distribution increases.

Hybrid classifications should be interpreted as mixed structural readings, not as ambiguity by default.

### Unclear / Early Signal

Unclear / Early Signal is used when the evidence is not yet strong enough to support a confident classification.

This can happen when the market is immature, the asset is changing rapidly, the role is not yet proven, or the available signals conflict.

An unclear result is not a failure. It is a disciplined refusal to force precision where the structure is still forming.

## Analytical Signals

### Infrastructure Capture

Infrastructure Capture evaluates how much demand an asset captures because other participants need its tools, systems, supply, platform, data, compute, distribution, or operational rails.

High Infrastructure Capture suggests a stronger Shovel or infrastructure-layer reading.

### Speculation Exposure

Speculation Exposure evaluates how dependent the asset is on a narrow narrative, visible winner thesis, hype cycle, or specific market outcome.

High Speculation Exposure often indicates a stronger Miner reading. Lower exposure can suggest the position benefits from broader system demand.

### Revenue Durability

Revenue Durability evaluates whether demand is recurring, embedded, operationally necessary, or difficult to replace.

Durable revenue signals may include repeated usage, switching friction, workflow dependence, capacity constraints, or long-term infrastructure need.

### Control Layer Strength

Control Layer Strength evaluates whether an asset shapes access, standards, permissions, distribution, pricing, production capacity, or market rules.

High Control Layer Strength supports a Gatekeeper or Control Layer reading.

### Classification Confidence

Classification Confidence indicates how clearly the available evidence supports the scanner output.

Confidence is not a claim of certainty. It is a directional judgment about signal quality. Low confidence should be treated as a warning that the asset may require more evidence, time, or case analysis.

## Interpreting Scanner Output

Scanner output should be read as a structured analytical prompt, not as a final verdict.

The result is useful when it helps a user ask better questions:

- Is this asset exposed to the visible race, or does it support many racers?
- Does the asset capture infrastructure demand?
- Does it depend on a narrow speculative outcome?
- Does it benefit from recurring or embedded need?
- Does it control access, standards, supply, pricing, or distribution?
- Is the evidence strong enough to classify, or is the signal still early?

The scanner is designed to clarify structural position. It is not designed to predict market price, rank investment attractiveness, or produce mechanical certainty.

## Directional Classification, Not False Precision

The Shovel Economy Scanner does not claim mathematical precision.

Markets are adaptive. Companies change strategy. Control can emerge or weaken. Infrastructure can become commoditized. A Miner can become a Shovel, and a Shovel can move toward Gatekeeper behavior.

For that reason, scanner outputs should be interpreted as directional classifications based on current structural signals. They are best used alongside the Framework and Dispatch archive, not as isolated conclusions.

## Relationship to the Framework

The Framework defines the taxonomy used by the scanner:

- Extraction Layer
- Infrastructure Layer
- Control Layer
- Miner
- Shovel
- Gatekeeper
- Hybrid
- Unclear / Early Signal

The scanner operationalizes that taxonomy by translating the concepts into applied signals a user can evaluate.

## Relationship to the Dispatch Archive

The Dispatch archive turns scanner-style classification into written analytical records.

Where the scanner provides an applied classification instrument, Dispatch entries preserve case-based reasoning. A dispatch should show why a classification holds, what signals matter, and where uncertainty remains.

Together, the Framework, Scanner, and Dispatch form a reference sequence:

Framework defines the model.  
Scanner applies the model.  
Dispatch records the model in real cases.
