🔧 SHOVEL 01
Bubble
AI-Powered No-Code App Builder
bubble.io

What it is: Bubble is an AI-powered no-code app builder for web and mobile. Its core advantage is that it combines visual building with built-in hosting, security, and performance features, which makes it one of the strongest platforms for turning an idea into a real product without writing traditional code.

Why it matters: Bubble sits on the product layer of the shovel economy. It reduces the distance between concept and launch. For founders, operators, and solo builders, that matters more than hype: it means you can validate faster, ship faster, and iterate without waiting on a full engineering team.

What you can build: AI-powered internal tools, niche SaaS products, client portals, intake systems, marketplaces, and lightweight workflow apps with AI features layered in.

Pricing signal: You can start building for free, then upgrade when you are ready to launch publicly.

🔧 SHOVEL 02
Make
Visual AI Workflow Automation
make.com

What it is: Make is a visual automation platform built for connecting apps, routing data, and orchestrating workflows. Its AI Toolkit can summarize, categorize, extract information, translate, identify language, standardize text, and more.

Why it matters: This is not just a no-code convenience layer. It is operational infrastructure. Make lets builders place AI in the middle of real business processes: intake, routing, enrichment, lead scoring, notifications, and structured outputs.

What makes it stronger in 2026: Make supports its own AI Provider across all plans, and paid plans can also connect custom providers such as OpenAI or Anthropic. It also integrates broadly across major AI and business apps, which makes it one of the most useful orchestration layers in the stack.

Pricing signal: Make offers a free plan with 1,000 credits per month and no time limit, with paid tiers above that.

🔧 SHOVEL 03
Voiceflow
No-Code AI Agent Builder for Support & Lead Generation
voiceflow.com

What it is: Voiceflow is a no-code platform for building, launching, and scaling AI agents for support, lead generation, and related customer-facing use cases.

Why it matters: Voiceflow is a practical shovel because it gives non-technical and cross-functional teams a way to move from concept to deployed conversational experience without owning the entire engineering burden themselves.

Operational proof: Voiceflow’s public site highlights customer-support and CX deployments, including case studies such as Turo and StubHub International, which it presents as examples of teams building and launching production agents on accelerated timelines.

Pricing signal: Voiceflow currently presents free sign-up / free trial entry points and usage-based billing paths, rather than the simple fixed-price consumer-style structure your earlier draft implied.
