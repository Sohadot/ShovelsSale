# Monetization Policy — ShovelsSale.com

## Purpose

This document defines the monetization policy for ShovelsSale.com.

ShovelsSale.com is a sovereign digital asset and reference system around the Shovel Economy. Monetization must never weaken its authority, credibility, strategic dignity, or long-term asset value.

Revenue is permitted only when it strengthens or preserves the asset.

The goal is not short-term extraction. The goal is durable value creation.

---

## 1. Governing Principle

Monetization must follow the doctrine:

Infrastructure creates durable value.

Therefore, ShovelsSale.com should monetize through products, placements, partnerships, tools, and research that align with infrastructure, market intelligence, strategic classification, and high-trust digital asset stewardship.

Any revenue model that damages trust is rejected, even if it generates short-term income.

---

## 2. Current Monetization Status

Status: Not yet active.

ShovelsSale.com is currently in the published and maintained stage. Monetization is planned only after the asset has sufficient governance, quality controls, SEO integrity, and reference authority.

Before monetization begins, the following must exist:

- `CLAUDE.md`
- `DECISION_LOG.md`
- `QUALITY_GATE.md`
- `SEO_POLICY.md`
- `MONETIZATION_POLICY.md`
- working security scan
- sitemap integrity
- metadata integrity
- clear revenue tracking plan
- no unresolved critical quality issues

## 3. Monetization Gate

No monetization element may be added unless it passes all five tests.

### 3.1 Authority Test

Does this revenue model strengthen or preserve the site’s authority?

Approved:

- serious research products
- infrastructure tool sponsorship
- premium scanner capabilities
- market maps
- analytical reports

Rejected:

- random banner ads
- irrelevant affiliate products
- low-trust offers
- intrusive popups

### 3.2 Doctrine Alignment Test

Does this revenue model align with the Shovel Economy?

Approved:

- tools used by builders, operators, analysts, investors, founders, and domain asset managers
- infrastructure software
- analytics tools
- security tools
- hosting and deployment tools
- AI infrastructure tools
- market intelligence products

Rejected:

- consumer gimmicks
- unrelated ecommerce products
- speculative “get rich quick” offers
- casino, adult, spam, or low-trust categories

### 3.3 User Trust Test

Would a serious visitor still trust the asset after seeing this monetization layer?

Approved:

- clearly disclosed sponsorship
- curated tools
- useful resources
- paid reports with real analytical value

Rejected:

- deceptive recommendations
- hidden sponsorship
- fake reviews
- excessive affiliate links
- misleading urgency

### 3.4 Long-Term Value Test

Does this revenue model increase long-term asset value?

Approved:

- revenue that creates data, subscribers, reports, reputation, or repeat use
- monetization that strengthens the reference system
- products that can become part of the asset’s institutional value

Rejected:

- short-term income that weakens perception
- cluttered ads
- low-quality sponsored posts
- paid backlink schemes
- anything that makes the asset look cheap

### 3.5 Operational Control Test

Can this revenue model be managed without damaging maintainability?

Approved:

- simple sponsorship blocks
- controlled directory listings
- downloadable reports
- measured affiliate resources
- premium tool access later

Rejected:

- complex ad stacks
- uncontrolled third-party widgets
- scripts that harm performance
- dependencies that weaken security posture

## 4. Forbidden Monetization

The following monetization models are prohibited:

- casino offers
- adult offers
- gambling links
- spam networks
- paid backlink schemes
- low-quality guest posts
- deceptive affiliate reviews
- fake product rankings
- intrusive popups
- auto-play video ads
- excessive display ads
- unrelated ecommerce ads
- irrelevant dropshipping offers
- crypto pump promotions
- make-money-fast offers
- paid content that does not disclose sponsorship
- any sponsorship that conflicts with the asset’s doctrine

These are prohibited because they weaken trust, reference value, and long-term asset dignity.

## 5. Approved Monetization Models

The following models are approved in principle, subject to quality review.

### 5.1 Shovel Economy Reports

Examples:

- The Shovel Economy Report
- AI Infrastructure Shovels Market Map
- Domain Names as Digital Infrastructure Assets
- Infrastructure Capture Index

Requirements:

- must contain original framing
- must have durable analytical value
- must be professionally presented
- must not be generic AI-generated content
- may be free, gated, or paid depending on maturity

### 5.2 Premium Scanner Features

The `/scanner/` can evolve into a premium classification tool.

Possible future features:

- Infrastructure Capture Score
- Speculation Exposure Score
- Revenue Durability Score
- Shovel / Miner / Gatekeeper classification
- Domain-as-Infrastructure assessment
- Downloadable classification report

Requirements:

- free version remains useful
- premium version adds real value
- no false precision
- methodology must be documented
- paid outputs must be credible

### 5.3 Selective Affiliate Resources

Affiliate monetization is allowed only when the product is relevant to infrastructure, building, analysis, operations, or digital asset stewardship.

Approved categories:

- hosting
- analytics
- SEO tools
- automation tools
- AI infrastructure tools
- security tools
- domain platforms
- no-code tools
- data tools
- research tools
- market intelligence platforms

Requirements:

- recommendations must be curated
- sponsorship or affiliate relationship must be disclosed
- tools must fit the doctrine
- no random affiliate stuffing
- no ranking manipulation

### 5.4 Dispatch Sponsorship

The Dispatch archive may support carefully selected sponsorship.

Approved sponsorship format:

`Supported by [Sponsor], an infrastructure tool for builders, operators, and analysts.`

Requirements:

- sponsorship must be disclosed
- sponsor must align with infrastructure, tools, analysis, or operations
- placement must be elegant and minimal
- sponsor must not control editorial conclusions

Rejected:

- loud banner ads
- unrelated offers
- sponsored claims disguised as analysis
- cluttered commercial blocks

### 5.5 Vendor Directory

A future directory may list tools and platforms aligned with the Shovel Economy.

Possible categories:

- AI Infrastructure
- Builder Tools
- Market Intelligence
- SEO Infrastructure
- Domain Infrastructure
- Automation Systems
- Security Infrastructure
- Data Platforms

Requirements:

- directory must be curated
- paid placement must be disclosed
- listings must meet quality standards
- no spam listings
- no unverified claims
- no mass low-quality submissions

### 5.6 Paid Research Products

Approved products:

- market maps
- industry reports
- classification briefs
- strategic asset reports
- domain infrastructure analysis
- founder opportunity briefs

Requirements:

- clear scope
- serious research posture
- no exaggerated financial promises
- no investment advice presented as certainty
- no unsupported claims

### 5.7 Newsletter Sponsorship

A future newsletter may be monetized through sponsorship.

Requirements:

- sponsor must align with the doctrine
- sponsorship must be disclosed
- audience trust must remain primary
- frequency must not become excessive
- newsletter must remain analytical, not promotional

## 6. Monetization Sequence

Monetization should follow this sequence:

1. Governance foundation
2. SEO and quality gate enforcement
3. Scanner improvement
4. Dispatch consistency
5. Lead magnet or free report
6. Newsletter or subscriber capture
7. Selective affiliate resources
8. Sponsorship or directory
9. Premium scanner or paid reports

This sequence prevents premature monetization.

---

## 7. Revenue Tracking

Before monetization begins, tracking should be defined.

Possible metrics:

- newsletter subscribers
- scanner completions
- report downloads
- affiliate clicks
- sponsor impressions
- directory clicks
- paid report sales
- return visitors
- organic traffic to reference pages

Tracking must respect privacy, performance, and trust.

---

## 8. Disclosure Policy

Any paid relationship must be disclosed clearly.

Disclosure examples:

- `This page may include selected partner links. We only include tools that align with the Shovel Economy framework.`
- `This dispatch is supported by [Sponsor]. Sponsorship does not influence the analytical classification.`
- `Featured listings may be sponsored. All listings remain subject to ShovelsSale.com quality standards.`

Hidden monetization is prohibited.

---

## 9. Design Standards for Monetization

Monetization elements must be visually restrained.

Allowed:

- subtle sponsorship block
- resource cards
- report callout
- professional directory listing
- minimal email capture
- elegant premium feature prompt

Rejected:

- aggressive popups
- flashing banners
- sticky ad clutter
- interruptive modals
- misleading buttons
- excessive commercial density

Monetization must feel like part of the reference system, not an invasion of it.

---

## 10. Editorial Independence

Sponsors, affiliates, or partners may not control:

- doctrine
- framework conclusions
- scanner methodology
- dispatch classification
- editorial judgment
- ranking logic
- strategic asset thesis

Revenue must support the asset, not govern it.

---

The following actions must be recorded in `DECISION_LOG.md`:

- first monetization activation
- first affiliate integration
- first sponsor acceptance
- first paid report launch
- first premium scanner feature
- directory launch
- major revenue model change
- rejection of a monetization model for doctrine reasons

Decision records should include:

- Date
- Decision
- Revenue model
- Rationale
- Risks
- Safeguards
- Status

---

## 12. Monetization Failure Conditions

A monetization model must be removed or revised if it causes:

- trust loss
- performance degradation
- SEO degradation
- user experience damage
- sponsor influence over analysis
- increased security risk
- conceptual downgrade
- brand cheapening
- conflict with Sovereign Asset System principles

---

## 13. Current Decision

ShovelsSale.com will not activate monetization until governance and quality gates are sufficiently formalized.

This policy prepares the asset for future revenue without allowing premature extraction.
